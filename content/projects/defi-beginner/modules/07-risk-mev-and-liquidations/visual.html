Module 07: Risk, MEV, and Liquidations

Execution-layer adversaries and why protocol safety is market-microstructure dependent

Why This Matters
  • Protocol logic can be correct while user outcomes are adversarially degraded.
  • MEV interacts directly with liquidation fairness and solvency protection.
  • Stress periods compress decision windows and amplify ordering games.

MEV is not a side topic. It is the mechanism that determines who captures residual value from ordering rights. In volatile windows, that residual value can be the difference between healthy liquidations and bad debt.

Worked Examples

Example 1: Liquidation bonus 5%, but slippage plus priority fees consume 4.2%. Net incentive is thin; fewer keepers participate under congestion.

Example 2: Private relay path reduces sandwich probability for large swaps, improving realized execution at cost of routing complexity.

Failure Modes
  • Searcher cartelization reduces competitive liquidation efficiency.
  • Oracle update predictability enables pre-positioned extraction.
  • Pause mechanisms trigger too late or can be griefed.
What To Monitor
  • Liquidation success rates by volatility regime.
  • Mempool-to-inclusion latency for critical transactions.
  • Observed sandwich frequency on major pools.
