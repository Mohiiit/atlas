Module 07 Deep Dive: MEV and Liquidation Risk

Execution-layer reality for DeFi protocols

What MEV Means Without Buzzwords

MEV is extra profit from controlling transaction ordering. If two people want same opportunity (arbitrage or liquidation), order decides winner.

This is similar to being first in line at a limited-stock sale.

Why It Matters for Beginners

You might think "my protocol math is correct, so I'm safe." Not enough. If execution layer is adversarial, users can still get poor fills and system can accumulate risk during congestion.

Typical Cases

  • Sandwich: attacker trades before and after victim to worsen victim execution.
  • Backrun: attacker captures predictable post-trade arbitrage.
  • Liquidation race: fastest bot wins liquidation bonus.

First-Principles Risk Question

Can your protocol remain solvent if keepers are delayed by congestion for N blocks during a 25% move?

If not, parameter safety is weaker than it appears.

What to Monitor

  1. Liquidation completion time during stress.
  2. Slippage and failed liquidations.
  3. Frequency of sandwich patterns on key pools.
