Module 03: Lending and Stablecoins

Pooled balance sheets, utilization-based rates, liquidation under stress, and peg defense

Why This Matters
  • Lending markets are core collateral infrastructure for DeFi leverage.
  • Stablecoins are the unit of account for most onchain activity.
  • Small parameter changes in rate curves or liquidation penalties can have systemic effects.
Mechanism Walkthrough
flowchart TD
A[Suppliers deposit]-->B[Pool liquidity]
B-->C[Borrow demand]
C-->D[Utilization rises/falls]
D-->E[Rate model updates APR]
E-->F[Behavior change: repay or supply]
F-->B
C-->G[Collateral health checks]
G-->H{Below threshold?}
H-->|yes|I[Liquidation]

Rate models steer equilibrium. Below the kink, APR grows gently; above the kink, APR rises sharply to prevent near-empty pool states.

Worked Examples

Example 1: Supply 100M, borrow 80M => utilization 80%. If kink is 85%, system near efficient zone.

Example 2: Utilization jumps to 95%; borrow APR spikes, suppliers are attracted, borrowers reduce demand, utilization mean-reverts if market responds.

Failure Modes
  • Borrow cap too high in illiquid collateral markets.
  • Liquidation discount too low to incentivize keepers.
  • Peg defense fails under simultaneous collateral and liquidity shocks.
What To Monitor
  • Utilization around kink over time.
  • Borrow concentration by asset and account.
  • Stablecoin peg deviation and redemption latency.
