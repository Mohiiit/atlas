Module 03 Deep Dive: Lending and Stablecoins

How pooled lending actually works and why stablecoins can depeg

Lending Pool Basics

Imagine many savers (like Maya) deposit funds into one shared pool. Borrowers (like Arjun) borrow from that pool. Interest paid by borrowers is distributed to depositors after protocol fees.

Unlike a fixed bank rate, DeFi rates often depend on utilization: how much of pool is borrowed right now.

Utilization Explained Like a Water Tank

If tank is almost full (low utilization), borrowing is easy, so rate can be lower. If tank is almost empty (high utilization), borrowing should be expensive to prevent exhaustion and attract new deposits.

This is why many protocols have a "kink" in rate curves: after a utilization threshold, rates rise sharply.

Stablecoin from Collateral

A protocol can let users lock collateral and mint a stablecoin. Example: Maya locks ETH worth 150 and mints 100 stable tokens. If ETH falls too much, system liquidates to keep minted stable tokens backed.

The peg (around $1) survives if users trust redemption paths and liquidation keeps system solvent.

Why Depeg Happens

  • Collateral drops faster than liquidations can clear.
  • Oracle delays cause late risk response.
  • Redemption path gets congested or capped.
  • Confidence shock: users all exit at once.

So "peg stability" is not only math. It is market depth, keeper speed, oracle quality, and user trust combined.

Beginner Checklist

  2. What collateral types back borrowed value?
  3. How quickly can risky positions be liquidated?
  4. What happens if a top collateral asset drops 30% in one hour?
