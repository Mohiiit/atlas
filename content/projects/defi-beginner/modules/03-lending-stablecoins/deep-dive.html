Module 03: Lending and Stablecoins (Beginner Deep Dive)

Mode: primer | Running example: Maya supplies, Arjun borrows

1. Why This Topic Exists

Not everyone needs to trade. Many users simply want to earn on idle assets or borrow without selling assets they hold long-term. Lending protocols and stablecoins provide this function onchain.

Analogy: a neighborhood credit pool: savers deposit, borrowers draw funds, rules enforce repayment safety.

2. Terms Before Mechanics

  • Supply: deposit assets into a lending pool to earn yield.
  • Borrow: take assets from pool and pay interest.
  • Utilization: borrowed amount divided by total supplied amount.
  • Kink model: interest curve that rises sharply past a threshold utilization.
  • Stablecoin: token designed to track a target value (often $1).
  • Depeg: stablecoin price deviates materially from target.

3. Running Example Setup

Maya deposits 100,000 USDC into a lending pool. Arjun borrows 60,000 USDC for business operations using collateral.

  • Total supply: 100,000
  • Total borrowed: 60,000
  • Utilization: 60%

4. Mechanism Walkthrough

  1. Suppliers deposit liquidity.
  2. Borrowers draw liquidity if collateral constraints pass.
  3. Protocol recomputes utilization continuously.
  4. Borrow APR adjusts by rate model.
  5. Borrowers repay with interest; suppliers earn yield share.
  6. If borrower health drops, liquidation reduces risk.

Who pays whom?

  • Borrowers pay interest.
  • Suppliers receive interest after protocol cut.
  • Liquidators earn discount/bonus for risk-off actions.

5. Worked Example A (Rate Dynamics)

Suppose kink at 80% utilization:

  • At 60%, borrow APR maybe 6%.
  • At 85%, borrow APR might jump to 14%.
  • At 95%, borrow APR might spike to 30%+.

Why? High utilization means little spare liquidity; higher rates discourage more borrowing and attract supply.

6. Worked Example B (Stablecoin Stress)

Arjun borrowed 60,000 against volatile collateral. If collateral value drops 25% quickly and liquidation is delayed:

  • Recovered collateral may not fully cover debt.
  • Pool can accumulate bad debt.
  • If stablecoin relies on this collateral quality, peg trust weakens.

This is how lending stress can spill into stablecoin stress.

7. Misconception Correction

Misconception: “Stablecoin at $1 today means it is always safe.”

Correction: peg stability is an ongoing process. It depends on collateral quality, liquidations, redemptions, and market trust.

8. Failure Modes

  • Technical: oracle lag misprices collateral.
  • Technical: liquidation bot downtime.
  • Economic: correlated collateral crash overwhelms liquidation capacity.
  • Economic: liquidity flight raises redemption pressure and widens depeg.

9. Protocol Evaluation Checklist

  1. What is utilization behavior during stress?
  2. How much liquidation volume can clear per block/minute?
  3. How diverse and liquid is collateral set?
  4. What are redemption guarantees for stablecoin users?

10. Check Your Understanding (With Answers)

  1. Why is high utilization risky?
    Pool has low spare liquidity and becomes fragile; rate spikes are needed to rebalance.
  2. What does liquidation prevent?
    Borrower insolvency from becoming protocol-wide bad debt.
  3. Can a stablecoin depeg without a code bug?
    Yes, through market stress, liquidity crunch, and confidence breakdown.
