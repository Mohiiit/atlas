Module 01: Money, Ledger, and Settlement (Beginner Deep Dive)

Mode: primer | Running example: Maya (saver) and Arjun (borrower)

1. Why This Topic Exists

The core problem is simple: one person has idle money, another person needs money now. In normal life, banks connect them. In DeFi, smart contracts do that job with public rules. If you understand this module, you can decode almost every DeFi system later.

House loan analogy: a bank gives you money because your house is collateral. In DeFi, you lock crypto instead of a house.

2. Term Unpacking (Plain First)

  • Collateral: safety asset locked to back your debt. Example: house in home loan, ETH in DeFi loan.
  • Borrow: take an existing asset and owe repayment.
  • Mint (in this context): protocol creates a token against your collateral (often stablecoin).
  • Settlement: final balance update onchain.
  • Liquidation: forced debt reduction when safety ratio gets too low.

Technical shorthand: many systems track collateral value / debt value. If this drops below threshold, liquidation can start.

3. Running Example Setup

Maya has ETH worth $15,000 and wants liquidity without selling ETH. Arjun is unrelated here; we will use him more in lending modules. Maya locks ETH worth $15,000 and borrows $10,000 stablecoins.

  • Initial collateral ratio: 15,000 / 10,000 = 150%
  • Protocol minimum safe ratio: 125%
  • Maya is safe initially.

4. Mechanism Walkthrough (State Changes)

  1. Deposit collateral: state records locked asset amount.
  2. Borrow/mint: state records debt amount.
  3. Price updates: oracle updates collateral valuation.
  4. Risk check: protocol recomputes ratio or health factor.
  5. If unsafe: liquidator repays part of debt and receives discounted collateral.

Who pays whom?

  • Borrower pays interest/fees.
  • Liquidator pays debt portion upfront and receives collateral discount reward.
  • Protocol may collect a fee slice.

5. Worked Example A (Normal Case)

Maya borrows $10,000 against $15,000 collateral. Market drops only 5%.

  • New collateral value: 15,000 * 0.95 = 14,250
  • New ratio: 14,250 / 10,000 = 142.5%
  • Still above 125%, so no liquidation.

Takeaway: small moves are tolerable because overcollateralization creates a buffer.

6. Worked Example B (Stress Case)

Now market drops 25% quickly.

  • New collateral value: 15,000 * 0.75 = 11,250
  • Ratio: 11,250 / 10,000 = 112.5%
  • Below 125%, liquidation eligible.

If liquidator repays $3,000 debt and gets collateral worth $3,300 (10% bonus), Maya debt falls but collateral also falls. This is painful for Maya but protects protocol solvency.

7. Common Misconception (Corrected)

Misconception: “If the smart contract is secure, my position is automatically safe.”

Correction: contract security is only one layer. You still face market risk, oracle risk, and liquidation timing risk.

8. Failure Modes (Technical vs Economic)

  • Technical: oracle outage delays ratio updates.
  • Technical: chain congestion delays liquidation transactions.
  • Economic: market falls faster than liquidators can absorb.
  • Economic: liquidation incentives too low to attract keepers.

9. Check Your Understanding (With Answers)

  1. Why require more collateral than debt?
    To absorb collateral volatility and keep debt likely recoverable.
  2. If ratio drops below threshold, what protects protocol first?
    Liquidation process, not borrower goodwill.
  3. Can a protocol be hacked if no code bug exists?
    Economically yes, through oracle manipulation, congestion, or poor incentives.

10. Summary in 5 Bullets

  • Collateral is a safety deposit backing debt.
  • DeFi settlement is public and rule-driven.
  • Overcollateralization buys time against volatility.
  • Liquidation is solvency machinery, not moral judgment.
  • Real safety depends on code + markets + execution.
