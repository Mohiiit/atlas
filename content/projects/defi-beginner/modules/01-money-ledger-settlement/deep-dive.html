Module 01 Deep Dive: Collateral, Debt, and Settlement

No finance background assumed

What Is Collateral in Daily Life?

Suppose you take a home loan. The bank gives you money because the house itself is collateral. If you stop paying, the bank can recover part of the loan by taking/selling the house. Collateral is therefore a safety asset backing a promise.

In DeFi, collateral is often crypto (ETH, BTC-like assets, liquid staking tokens). You lock it in a smart contract to borrow something else.

Our Running Example

Maya deposits 150 units of value as collateral and borrows 100 units. Her initial collateral ratio is 150 / 100 = 150%. If protocol minimum is 125%, she is safe for now.

Now market falls. Collateral value drops from 150 to 120. Ratio becomes 120 / 100 = 120%. She is below minimum. Position can be liquidated.

What Liquidation Really Means

Liquidation is forced risk reduction, not punishment for its own sake. A third party pays part of Maya's debt and receives part of her collateral at a discount. This discount is the economic incentive to keep the system solvent.

If no one liquidates in time, debt can exceed recoverable collateral. That is bad debt, and system health deteriorates.

Settlement and Finality (Simple View)

Settlement means balances are actually updated onchain. Finality means those updates are effectively irreversible after enough block confirmations.

If a system depends on fast reaction but chain is congested, settlement delay itself becomes risk.

Key Terms in This Module

  • Borrow: take an existing asset and owe it back.
  • Mint: create a new protocol token against collateral.
  • Collateral Ratio: collateral value divided by debt value.
  • Liquidation Threshold: minimum safe ratio.
  • Bad Debt: debt that remains after collateral is exhausted.
