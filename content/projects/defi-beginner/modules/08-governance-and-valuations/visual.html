Module 08: Governance and Valuation

Control rights, cash-flow rights, and parameter governance risk

Why This Matters
  • Token value depends on who captures protocol cash flow and how credibly that policy is maintained.
  • Governance quality directly affects risk premium and therefore valuation multiples.
  • Delegation and upgrade-key structure matter as much as fee headline numbers.

Governance is a capital allocation engine. The same gross fee line can produce very different token outcomes depending on distribution policy, treasury discipline, and dilution schedule.

Worked Examples

Example 1: Net fees 120M, 35% to stakers => 42M to holders. With 300M circulating, annual flow/token = 0.14.

Example 2: If governance shifts to 20% distribution, flow/token falls to 0.08, potentially compressing valuation unless offset by growth expectations.

Failure Modes
  • Low participation allows concentrated actors to control policy.
  • Short-term incentive votes harm long-term solvency.
  • Admin key centralization undermines decentralization premium.
What To Monitor
  • Vote participation and delegate concentration.
  • Treasury runway and spend efficiency.
  • Real tokenholder cash flow vs headline fee metrics.
