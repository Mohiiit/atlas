Module 05: Prediction Markets

Content-rich explainer: probability pricing, market design, oracle resolution, and manipulation resistance

Why This Matters
  • Prediction markets turn belief into price, offering measurable expectations instead of narrative-only opinions.
  • They are useful only when market design and resolution logic are robust against ambiguity and manipulation.
  • For builders, the oracle and dispute mechanism is the true trust anchor, not the trading UI.
  • Understanding this mechanism helps you evaluate both information quality and protocol risk.

Trading mechanics are usually straightforward. The hard part is unambiguous resolution. If outcome criteria are vague, the market price can look precise while being economically meaningless near expiry.

A high-quality market needs three properties together: precise question wording, transparent truth source, and anti-spam dispute economics.

First Principles
  1. Price as probability: in liquid, low-friction markets, YES price approximates expected probability under current beliefs.
  2. Expected value governs edge: profitable trade requires your belief-adjusted expected payout to exceed price + fees.
  3. Resolution is settlement layer: the market is a derivative on the adjudication process.
  4. Liquidity quality matters: thin books distort probability interpretation and amplify manipulation near close.
Worked Examples

Example 1: implied probability trade
YES token at 0.63. Buying 1,000 shares costs 630. If YES resolves true, payout is 1,000 and gross profit is 370. Break-even probability before fees is 63%.

Example 2: edge with your own belief
If your calibrated model gives 70% and market is 63%, expected value per share is roughly 0.70 - 0.63 = 0.07 before fees/slippage. Positive edge exists only if execution costs are below that spread.

Failure Modes
  • Ambiguous market specification: participants disagree on what exactly is being resolved.
  • Late-stage liquidity vacuum: small capital can swing displayed probability near expiry.
  • Oracle dispute capture: attacker economically dominates reporting path.
  • Wash-volume distortion: fake flow creates false confidence in price signal quality.
What To Monitor
  • Bid-ask spread and depth at key price levels.
  • Volume concentration near finalization window.
  • Dispute frequency, duration, and outcome reversal rate.
  • Difference between final market probability and realized event frequency over time (calibration).
  • Share of volume from repeat counterparties (wash-trade warning signal).
