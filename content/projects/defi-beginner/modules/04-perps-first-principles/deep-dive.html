Module 04 Deep Dive: Perpetual Markets

From "what is leverage" to liquidation and insurance

Leverage in Daily Language

Leverage means controlling a bigger position with smaller own capital. If Maya has 6,000 and opens a 60,000 position, leverage is 10x. Gains and losses both scale up.

At 10x, a 5% adverse move can wipe about half her collateral. So leverage is speed, not free profit.

What Is a Perpetual Contract?

A perp is like a future that never expires. Since there is no expiry settlement date, exchanges use funding payments to keep perp price near spot market price.

If perp trades too expensive, longs may pay shorts. This motivates balance and pulls price closer to spot over time.

Mark Price, Not Last Trade Price

Liquidation usually uses mark/index-based price, not random last trade print. This reduces manipulation from tiny malicious trades, but quality depends on oracle/index design.

Running Example

Maya longs 1 BTC at 60,000 with 6,000 collateral.

  • If price goes to 57,000, PnL = -3,000.
  • New equity = 3,000.
  • If maintenance requirement is 3,500, liquidation starts.

Funding cost also reduces equity over time even if price is flat.

Insurance Fund and ADL

If liquidation cannot close a position at expected price, shortfall appears. Insurance fund absorbs that first. If fund is insufficient, systems may auto-deleverage profitable opposing traders (ADL) or use other socialized mechanisms.

This is why "who absorbs tail loss" is a key evaluation question.

Beginner Evaluation Questions

  1. How is mark price computed?
  2. How fast can liquidations execute in a crash?
  3. How large is insurance relative to open interest?
  4. What exact rule is used when insurance is depleted?
