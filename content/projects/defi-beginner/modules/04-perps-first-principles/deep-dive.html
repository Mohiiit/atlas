Module 04: Perpetual Markets (Beginner Deep Dive)

Mode: primer | Running example: Maya trades BTC perp

1. Why This Topic Exists

People want to bet on price direction with leverage, without owning full asset amount. Perpetuals provide this continuously (no expiry date), which is why they dominate crypto derivatives volume.

Analogy: driving a car with nitro boost. You go faster, but mistakes become much more expensive.

2. Terms You Must Know First

  • Leverage: position size divided by your collateral.
  • Collateral/Margin: capital backing your open position.
  • Mark Price: reference price used for risk and liquidation.
  • Funding Rate: periodic payment between longs and shorts to keep perp near spot.
  • Liquidation: forced closure when equity is too low.
  • Insurance Fund: buffer for liquidation shortfalls.

3. Running Example Setup

Maya has $6,000 collateral and opens a long BTC perp position worth $60,000 (10x).

  • Entry price: $60,000
  • Position size: 1 BTC
  • Maintenance threshold: assume equity must stay above $3,500

4. Mechanism Walkthrough

  1. Trader opens position with collateral.
  2. Mark price updates continuously.
  3. Unrealized PnL updates: size * (mark - entry).
  4. Equity updates: collateral + PnL - fees - funding.
  5. If equity below maintenance, liquidation engine closes all/part of position.
  6. If close price insufficient, insurance fund covers shortfall.

5. Worked Example A (Price Move)

Price drops from $60,000 to $57,000.

  • PnL = 1 * (57,000 - 60,000) = -3,000
  • Equity = 6,000 - 3,000 = 3,000
  • Since 3,000 < 3,500 maintenance, liquidation can trigger.

6. Worked Example B (Funding Drag)

Assume funding is +0.01% every 8h (longs pay shorts), notional = $60,000.

  • Payment per interval = 60,000 * 0.0001 = 6
  • After 30 intervals (~10 days): 6 * 30 = 180

Even with flat price, this funding cost slowly reduces equity.

7. Misconception Correction

Misconception: “If price comes back later, high leverage is fine.”

Correction: you can be liquidated before recovery. Survival path matters more than eventual direction.

8. Failure Modes

  • Technical: mark/index feed failure causes wrong liquidations.
  • Economic: rapid move + low liquidity => liquidation slippage and shortfalls.
  • Systemic: insurance fund depletion can trigger ADL/socialized loss.
  • Operational: chain congestion delays risk-off actions.

9. Beginner Checklist for Comparing Perp Protocols

  1. How mark price is computed?
  2. Liquidation speed under stress?
  3. Insurance fund size relative to open interest?
  4. Policy when insurance is insufficient?

10. Check Your Understanding (With Answers)

  1. What is the key danger of high leverage?
    Small adverse moves can push equity below maintenance quickly.
  2. Why funding exists?
    To keep perp price anchored near spot over time.
  3. Can you lose even if final market direction is correct?
    Yes, if interim move liquidates you before reversal.
