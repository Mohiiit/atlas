Module 06: Options and Structured DeFi

Payoff engineering, premium income, and hidden tail-risk transfer

Why This Matters
  • Many "yield" products are options positions wrapped in simpler UX.
  • Options expose non-linear risk that cannot be understood with APR-only views.
  • Structured vaults can underperform badly in trends despite smooth historical returns.
Mechanism Walkthrough
flowchart TD
A[Deposit underlying asset]-->B[Vault sells options]
B-->C[Collect premium]
C-->D[Expiry settlement]
D-->E{Spot above strike?}
E-->|no|F[Keep underlying + premium]
E-->|yes|G[Upside capped]
F-->H[Yield distribution]
G-->H

Covered call vaults monetize upside uncertainty by selling convexity. Premium is not free yield; it is compensation for capping upside and bearing assignment path risk.

Worked Examples

Example 1: Sell call strike 2,500 for 80 premium on 1 ETH. If expiry 2,300, strategy outperforms holding by +80.

Example 2: If expiry 2,900, strategy misses much of rally beyond strike; "yield" can underperform spot strongly.

Failure Modes
  • Vol regime shift makes historical premium assumptions invalid.
  • Liquidity gaps widen implied-vol spreads and execution cost.
  • Vault roll logic executes at poor times, creating negative convexity drag.
What To Monitor
  • Realized vs implied volatility gap.
  • Option sale timing quality and slippage.
  • Strategy payoff relative to passive benchmark in up/down/chop regimes.
