Module 06: Options and Structured DeFi (Beginner Deep Dive)

Mode: primer | Running example: Maya uses covered-call vault

1. Why This Topic Exists

Many users want “higher yield” than simple lending. Structured products offer this by packaging option strategies. The tradeoff: smoother income often comes from taking hidden downside or capped upside.

Analogy: renting out your car for income. You earn rent, but give up flexibility and accept wear/risk.

2. Terms Before Mechanics

  • Option: contract granting right to buy/sell at pre-set price.
  • Call option: right to buy asset at strike price.
  • Strike: fixed exercise price.
  • Premium: upfront payment for option.
  • Covered call: own asset and sell call on it.
  • Implied volatility: market's expected volatility embedded in option price.

3. Running Example Setup

Maya owns 1 ETH. She deposits into a vault that sells monthly call option at strike 2,500 and collects premium 80 USDC.

4. Mechanism Walkthrough

  1. Users deposit underlying asset.
  2. Vault sells options periodically.
  3. Vault receives premium and tracks liabilities.
  4. At expiry, settlement depends on spot vs strike.
  5. Vault distributes resulting performance to depositors.

5. Worked Example A (Calm/Flat Market)

ETH settles at 2,300 (below strike 2,500).

  • Call expires unexercised.
  • Maya keeps ETH exposure.
  • Premium 80 is retained as extra return.

6. Worked Example B (Strong Uptrend)

ETH settles at 2,900 (above strike).

  • Upside above strike mostly given away due to call obligation.
  • Maya earns premium but underperforms simply holding ETH in strong rally.

Lesson: premium income is payment for giving away some upside.

7. Misconception Correction

Misconception: “Option vault APY is free yield.”

Correction: yield comes from selling future upside or taking convexity risk. It is compensation, not free money.

8. Failure Modes

  • Technical: bad strike selection logic or execution bugs.
  • Market: violent uptrend causes repeated opportunity loss.
  • Liquidity: option market depth poor at roll time.
  • Model risk: volatility assumptions break during regime shift.

9. Protocol Evaluation Checklist

  1. How are strike and expiry selected?
  2. How transparent is payoff decomposition?
  3. How does strategy perform vs spot in uptrend/downtrend/chop?
  4. What safeguards exist around roll execution quality?

10. Check Your Understanding (With Answers)

  1. What is the income source in covered-call vaults?
    Option premium from selling call options.
  2. When does strategy most clearly underperform spot?
    Strong upside trends where upside is capped by sold calls.
  3. Why can smooth backtests be misleading?
    They may not include harsh volatility regime shifts and tail paths.
