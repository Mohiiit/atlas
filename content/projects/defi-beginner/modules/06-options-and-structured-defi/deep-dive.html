Module 06 Deep Dive: Options and Structured DeFi

Why "yield" products often hide option risk

Option in One Sentence

An option gives right (not obligation) to buy/sell an asset at a fixed price by a given time. Option seller gets premium now and takes contingent risk later.

Covered Call Example (Beginner-Friendly)

Maya owns 1 ETH. She sells a call option with strike 2,500 and receives premium 80.

  • If ETH ends at 2,300: option not exercised; she keeps ETH + 80.
  • If ETH ends at 2,900: upside above strike is mostly sacrificed.

So extra yield comes from giving up some upside potential.

What Structured Vaults Do

Structured DeFi products bundle repeated option actions into one vault UX. Good for simplicity, but you must still understand underlying payoff shape. Smooth historical chart can hide large tail risk.

Beginner Terms

  • Strike: fixed exercise price.
  • Expiry: option end date.
  • Premium: upfront price paid for option.
  • Implied Volatility: market's expected future volatility, embedded in option price.

Questions Before Using a Vault

  1. Where exactly does yield come from?
  2. In which scenario does strategy underperform spot heavily?
  3. How often and how is strike reset?
