First-Principles Glossary for DeFi Beginners

Use this before each module. Every term is explained in plain language first, then in protocol language.

Collateral

Plain language: collateral is something valuable you lock as a safety deposit when you borrow money. In real life, your house is collateral in a home loan. If you stop paying, the lender can take the house to recover money.

In DeFi: you lock assets (for example ETH) to borrow or mint another asset (for example stablecoins). If your collateral value drops too much, your position can be liquidated.

Borrowing vs Minting

Borrow: you take an existing asset from a pool and promise to return it with interest.

Mint: protocol creates a new token against your collateral (common in stablecoin systems). You later repay/burn to unlock collateral.

Liquidation

Plain language: forced closure when your safety margin is too low.

In DeFi: if collateral ratio or health factor falls below threshold, third-party liquidators repay part of your debt and take discounted collateral.

AMM (Automated Market Maker)

Plain language: a vending machine for trading assets. You do not need a direct buyer/seller; you trade against a pool.

In DeFi: smart contract pool quotes prices using a formula (for example constant product x*y=k).

Slippage

Plain language: the difference between expected price and actual execution price.

Why it happens: your trade changes pool balance; larger trades move price more.

Perpetual Futures (Perps)

Plain language: leveraged bet on price direction with no expiry date.

Core parts: collateral, mark price, funding payments, liquidation engine.

Funding Rate

Plain language: periodic payment between long and short traders to keep perp price near spot price.

If perps trade above spot, longs often pay shorts. If below spot, shorts often pay longs.

Prediction Market

Plain language: market where tokens represent outcomes of future events (YES/NO).

Price is often interpreted as probability, but only if market has enough liquidity and clear resolution rules.

Oracle

Plain language: data feed from outside blockchain (prices, event results).

Risk: if oracle is late, wrong, or manipulable, protocol can make bad liquidations or wrong settlements.

MEV (Maximal Extractable Value)

Plain language: extra profit from controlling transaction ordering in a block.

Common forms: sandwich attacks, liquidation racing, arbitrage backruns.

Health Factor / Collateral Ratio

These are risk meters. They compare your collateral value to borrowed value. When they go below a protocol threshold, liquidation is triggered.

Uniswap v4 Hook

Plain language: custom plugin logic that runs around a swap or liquidity action.

Benefit: dynamic fees or custom behavior. Risk: more complexity and more attack surface.
